As AI systems become more integrated into decision-making processes, it's critical that engineers understand the ethical implications, identify and mitigate bias, and implement explainability techniques to ensure transparency, accountability, and fairness.

✅ Topics Covered

1. AI Ethics & Responsible AI
   Principles of ethical AI: fairness, transparency, accountability, privacy, security
   Risks of unchecked AI deployment (e.g., discrimination, misinformation)
   Ethical design frameworks (e.g., EU AI Act, IEEE Ethically Aligned Design)
   Role of Responsible AI teams and practices in modern organizations
1. Bias in Machine Learning
   Types of bias: selection bias, label bias, measurement bias, etc.
   How bias enters data and models (e.g., historical bias, confirmation bias)
   Techniques to identify, measure, and mitigate bias:
   Fairness metrics (e.g., disparate impact, demographic parity)
   Bias detection tools (e.g., IBM AI Fairness 360, Fairlearn)
1. Explainability & Interpretability
   Why explainability matters (especially in regulated domains: finance, healthcare)
   Interpretable models vs. black-box models
   Post-hoc explainability techniques:
   SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
   LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)
   Feature importance visualizations and counterfactual explanations
1. Model Governance & Auditing
   Versioning, tracking decisions, and data/model lineage
   Audit trails, accountability layers, and human-in-the-loop systems
   Transparency reporting for AI systems
